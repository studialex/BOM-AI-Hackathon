# iknl.nl

## What is this source?
The website https://www.iknl.nl is the public website of **IKNL (Integraal Kankercentrum Nederland)**, the Netherlands Comprehensive Cancer Organisation.  
IKNL is responsible for collecting, analysing, and disseminating data and knowledge about cancer and palliative care in the Netherlands, and for supporting healthcare professionals, policymakers, and researchers.

The website contains:
- Information about cancer incidence, prevalence, survival, and trends
- Methodological documentation and reports
- Disease-specific background information
- News, updates, and publications related to oncology and palliative care

## Navigating the website
The iknl.nl website contains a large amount of valuable information, but it can be **difficult to navigate**, especially for first-time users:
- Content is spread across reports, dashboards, news items, and background pages
- Many pages are written for professional or policy audiences
- Data and context are often separated across multiple sections

For this hackathon, it is recommended to start from one of the entry points below instead of browsing the site at random.

## Key entry points

### 1. Netherlands Cancer Registry (NKR)
https://iknl.nl/nkr

This section explains the **Netherlands Cancer Registry**, one of IKNL’s core assets.  
Here you can find:
- Information on how cancer data is collected and registered
- What variables are available (diagnosis, stage, treatment, outcomes, etc.)
- Descriptions of data quality and methodology

This is the most relevant entry point for teams interested in **population-level cancer data** or data-driven insights.

---

### 2. Cancer types
https://iknl.nl/kankersoorten

This section provides structured background information per cancer type, typically from a **professional or epidemiological perspective**, such as:
- Disease characteristics
- Incidence and trends
- High-level treatment and outcome context

This can be useful for professionals wanting insights on the burden of specific types of cancer.

---

### 3. News and publications
https://iknl.nl/nieuws

This section contains:
- News items
- Research updates and publications
- Policy-relevant insights and trends

These pages are useful if you want to:
- Track recent developments in oncology and cancer care
- Explore how research findings are communicated to a broader audience

## How to work with this source (hackathon context)
For this hackathon, we deliberately **do not prescribe a single technical access method** for iknl.nl.

Depending on the team’s goals, you may choose to:
- Read and interpret content manually as background or context
- Scrape selected public pages or reports (responsibly and at small scale)
- Combine insights from iknl.nl with other sources (e.g. such as kanker.nl)

Always keep in mind:
- The professional and policy-oriented nature of the content
- The absence of a public API for most information
- Responsible and transparent use of publicly available material
