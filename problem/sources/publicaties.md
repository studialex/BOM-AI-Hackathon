# Publications IKNL

## What is this source?
All official IKNL publications can be found on the IKNL website: https://iknl.nl/onderzoek/publicaties

The Netherlands Comprehensive Cancer Organisation (IKNL) generates high‑quality scientific insights based on data from the Netherlands Cancer Registry (NKR). These insights are published as peer‑reviewed scientific articles and reports covering:

- Trends and developments in cancer care in the Netherlands
- Societal issues related to cancer
- Outcomes, diagnostics, and treatments for specific cancer types

Together, these publications form an important evidence base for researchers, clinicians, policymakers, and the wider oncology community.
<br><br>

## Scientific Publications
IKNL has many scientific publications, but its difficult to navigate them.
- They are listed under “Overzicht wetenschappelijke publicaties”
- Articles span many years, cancer types, and research themes
- Not all publications are open access
- Metadata and filtering options for bulk exploration or analysis are non-existing

## Reports
In addition to scientific journal publications, IKNL publishes a wide range of **reports** that translate registry data into more comprehensive analyses. These reports are typically aimed at policymakers, healthcare professionals, and other stakeholders in cancer care.

The reports can be broadly grouped into three categories:

- **General cancer reports**  
  Overarching reports describing cancer incidence, survival, mortality, and care trends in the Netherlands.

- **Tumour‑specific reports**  
  In‑depth analyses focusing on a single cancer type or tumour group.

- **Thematic reports**  
  Reports addressing cross‑cutting themes such as socioeconomic status, healthcare accessibility, variation in care, or the use of expensive medicines.

Older reports are published as **PDF documents**, newer ones are presented as **dedicated web pages** that combine narrative, figures, and interactive elements.

> **Hackathon note**  
> Reports often contain rich contextual information, visualisations, and summaries that are not always available in journal articles.

**All reports** can be found at https://iknl.nl/onderzoek/publicaties under "Rapporten over kanker", "Kanker in Nederland: socialeconomische verschillen", "Dure Geneesmiddelen", and "Rapporten over kankersoorten (specifiek)".
